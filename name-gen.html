How It Works

Step 1: Roll d100 for the number of syllables. Syllables can be joined to form a word (50%), or separated by spaces (37.5% chance) and hyphens (12.5% chance).

01-10 One syllable; 11-70 Two syllables; 71-90 Three syllables; 91-100 Four syllables.

Step 2: Roll 1d6 for each syllable to determine the table to use. Repeat this step for each new syllable. 1-2: "A"; 3-4: "B"; 5-6: "C"

Step 3: Roll d100 to determine the syllable from the list. Repeat Steps 2 and 3 until each syllable is determined.

Based on a B/X D&D Name Generator that Eric sent me. Each of these syllables was collected from a proper name from the B/X Rulebooks and B/X modules, or was from the name of an early D&D author.

Note: I added a fourth table with my own syllables. Just to keep things fun and funky fresh, y'know?
